# Recommendations

4Ps marketing strategy per segment, based on the insights in
[ANALYSIS.md](./ANALYSIS.md).

## Segment Priority

| Priority | Segment | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 🥇 1 | Professional | Highest revenue at risk, mid–high spending power, low promo reliance |
| 🥈 2 | Student | Largest audience, strong seasonality can drive high-volume recovery |
| 🥉 3 | Parent | Loyal, stable base — worth retaining rather than aggressively targeting |
| 4 | Teacher | Small, unresponsive to promotions — low priority |
| 5 | Retiree | Small but growing revenue per order — premium opportunity, not volume |

## Professional

| Element | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Place | Concentrate on Online (38%), Queensland (20%), and Victoria (18%) |
| Product | Fiction, Textbook, Non-Fiction — knowledge + leisure titles |
| Promotion | Loyalty perks (free shipping, points) instead of heavy discounts; promote professional-growth bundles |
| Price | Target $0–50 (65%) and $100–150 (19%); emphasize value and credibility |
| Timing | Quarterly pay cycles — March, June, September, December |

## Student

| Element | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Place | Focus on New South Wales (37%) and Online (29%) |
| Product | Textbooks, Non-Fiction, Fiction for study and leisure |
| Promotion | Student10 / Member15 codes, tied to seasonal "Back to School" and exam-period sales |
| Price | Emphasize $0–50 (73%) and $50–100; bundle low-cost books for affordability |
| Timing | January–February (semester start) and July–August (mid-year) |

## Parent

| Element | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Place | Online (24%), Victoria (24%), NSW (22%) |
| Product | Textbooks (30%), Non-Fiction, Fiction — school-related and parenting content |
| Promotion | Back-to-school bundles, family loyalty points, referral rewards |
| Price | Emphasize $0–100 range |
| Timing | January–February (school start) and September (term refresh) |

## Teacher

| Element | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Place | Online (53%), with presence in NSW and Victoria (35%) |
| Product | Children's, Fiction |
| Promotion | Teacher-exclusive programs, modest perks (e.g. 10% discount, free delivery) |
| Price | Center on under $50 (71%) and $50–100 (19%) |
| Timing | January (new school year) and July (term break) |

## Retiree

| Element | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Place | Online (38%) and Victoria (23%) |
| Product | Fiction, Non-Fiction, Textbooks |
| Promotion | Minimal discounts; emphasize quality, comfort, curated reading sets |
| Price | $0–50 (61%) and $100–150 (19%) — mid-tier and premium bundles |
| Timing | Soft campaigns in April–May and October–November (off-peak) |

## Answering the Business Questions

**How bad is the situation?** → Present the YoY decline across orders, sales,
and profit to the CEO as the case for urgent marketing investment.

**Which segments to target?** → Reallocate budget to Professional and
Student first — they carry the largest absolute revenue loss and the
clearest recovery upside.

**What to actually run?** → Three campaign briefs, each matched to a
segment's channel, product mix, price sensitivity, and seasonal timing —
ready to scope for budget and ROI in the next planning stage:
- **"Learn & Grow"** (Professional) — LinkedIn/Google Ads, business and
  self-development titles, loyalty rewards over discounts
- **"Back-to-School Smart Savings"** (Student) — social/university channels,
  10–15% bundle discounts
- **"Family Reading Month"** (Parent) — bundle deals on educational and
  children's books
